## Theory of the Case

This is an independent action in equity to set aside the final judgment entered in CGC-21-594102
on the grounds of **extrinsic fraud** that deprived Plaintiff of a fair opportunity to present
material evidence and fairly litigate liability.

**Authority:** Code Civ. Proc. § 187; inherent equitable power; *Kulchar v. Kulchar* (1969) 1 Cal.3d 467.

### The Three-Leg Framework of Extrinsic Fraud

1. **Defense-side knowledge and access** — Defendants had possession of and access to the
   critical evidence materials (911 call, body camera footage, CAD logs) through prior
   receipt and production history.

2. **False and misleading representations to the Court** — Defense made false and misleading
   representations about receipt and prior production, including false denials of knowledge
   and false claims of "unknown provenance."

3. **Court reliance** — The Court relied on those representations to deny continuances,
   exclude materials, and defer admission, creating concrete procedural barriers that
   prevented Plaintiff from fairly presenting the evidence.

### The Critical Evidence

- **911 call recording:** Defendant Abdelhalim stated *"I barely hit him with anything."*
  Plaintiff's former counsel obtained this through a routine public records request within
  three months of the collision. CSAA never obtained it before issuing its denial.
- **Body-worn camera footage:** Contained eyewitness statements material to liability.
- **CAD dispatch logs:** Contemporaneous record of the incident.

## Relief Sought

1. **Equitable vacatur** of the judgment in CGC-21-594102
2. **Retrial** on a fair, complete, and accurate evidentiary record
3. **Ancillary equitable monetary relief** (restitutionary; bounded to the fraud-driven period):
   - Out-of-pocket costs for emergency foundation work and record reconstruction
   - Incremental medical/therapy/pharmacy expenses tied to the fraud period
   - Time and opportunity costs caused by the process corruption
