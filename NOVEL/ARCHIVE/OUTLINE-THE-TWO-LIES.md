## PART ONE: THE COLLISION

### The Empirical Foundation

On the afternoon of February 4, 2021, in San Francisco, a vehicle driven by Subhi Abdelhalim struck Franciscus Dylan Rosario while Rosario was walking his scooter through a crosswalk. What followed was not an unusual event in American civil litigation. Thousands of pedestrian-vehicle collision cases are filed every year in California. What made this case singular was not the collision itself, nor the injuries, nor even the brain damage Rosario sustained. What made it singular was the architecture of lies that two institutions -- an insurance company and its retained law firms -- constructed around that collision over the next four years, the way those lies weaponized the court system against the very person the system was designed to protect, and the forensic precision with which one man, operating alone and brain-injured, deconstructed the fraud from the certified record and turned his adversary's own words into an inescapable trap.

This is a true story. Every factual claim in this outline is drawn from the certified Reporter's Transcript of the Superior Court of California, County of San Francisco; from filed court documents bearing case numbers CGC-21-594102, CGC-25-631801, CGC-25-631802, and A173827; from sworn deposition testimony; from the official Clerk's Transcript; and from public records. The transcript is the spine of the narrative. What appears on that transcript cannot be altered, withdrawn, or recharacterized. Written words endure.

## THE CAST

### The Plaintiff

**Franciscus Dylan Rosario** is a senior AI systems architect whose professional trajectory, before February 4, 2021, placed him at the intersection of artificial intelligence, distributed systems, and enterprise software engineering. He was a highly qualified senior AI Architect in the San Francisco tech market. He is the son of immigrants. He built his career through technical mastery, not institutional connections. He is the kind of person the American civil justice system was designed for: someone without inherited power or corporate backing who relies on the courts to provide a level field when a wrong has been committed against him.

The traumatic brain injury Rosario sustained in the collision did not make him less intelligent. It made his intelligence harder to deploy. A TBI degrades executive function -- the ability to plan, sequence, prioritize, and sustain focus across complex tasks. For a man whose livelihood depended on precisely those cognitive capacities, the injury was occupationally catastrophic. He could still think. He could still analyze. He could still write with precision and construct legal arguments with the rigor of a trained litigator. But the overhead cost of every cognitive act had increased by an order of magnitude, and the sustained stamina required to manage a multi-year, multi-front litigation against institutional opponents with unlimited resources and professional teams of lawyers was the daily reality of his life after the collision.

When the Dolan Law Firm withdrew from his case, Rosario did what almost no one in his position does: he continued the litigation himself, in pro per -- without a lawyer, against one of the largest automobile insurers in California and two retained defense firms. He did this not because he wanted to. He did it because the system had already failed him once, and he was not going to let it fail him again without a fight.

### The Defendant

**Subhi Abdelhalim** is the driver who struck Rosario. In the minutes after the collision, while standing at the scene with the adrenaline still in his blood and the police arriving, Abdelhalim called 911. On that call, captured by the dispatch system and preserved as a public record, Abdelhalim said four words that would define the next five years of litigation: **"I barely hit him."**

Those four words are what the law calls a party admission under Evidence Code section 1220. They are not hearsay. They are a defendant's own statement, admissible against him as a matter of right. They are the single most powerful category of evidence in civil litigation because they come from the defendant's own mouth, in the defendant's own words, at the moment closest to the event. When Abdelhalim later took the stand at trial and testified that "no contact" occurred -- that he never struck Rosario -- his own 911 call recording stood as a direct, authenticated contradiction. He eventually admitted on the stand: "Yes. That is my voice."

Abdelhalim is not the villain of this story. He is the person whose insurance company decided to deny a valid claim twenty-one days after the collision, and whose lawyers then lied to a judge to prevent the jury from hearing the truth. He is the beneficiary of a fraud he may not have directed but from which he directly profited. The defense verdict that bears his name was procured through the corruption of the court.

### The Insurance Company

**CSAA Insurance Exchange** is a multi-billion-dollar automobile liability insurer and a member of the AAA federation. It is the entity that selected, retained, and funded every lawyer who appeared on behalf of the defense in this case. It is the entity that issued the denial letter. It is the entity that paid for the motions in limine. It is the entity that paid for the hearing representations. It is the entity that paid for the trial. CSAA is the principal. Every act its agents committed was committed in CSAA's name, with CSAA's money, for CSAA's benefit.

Twenty-one days after the collision, on February 25, 2021, CSAA mailed Rosario a denial letter. The letter stated that CSAA had "concluded" its investigation and determined that its insured was not liable. The word "concluded" carries an implied factual predicate: the investigation is finished because the investigative steps have been performed. A reasonable person receiving that letter from a multi-billion-dollar institutional insurer understands "concluded investigation" to mean that the basic steps of an automobile liability investigation -- obtaining 911 recordings, body-worn camera footage, computer-aided dispatch records -- have been performed and reviewed.

CSAA had not obtained the 911 recording. CSAA had not obtained the body-worn camera footage. CSAA had not obtained the CAD dispatch records. Rosario's own former counsel, the Dolan Law Firm, had obtained the 911 recording through a routine public records request within three months of the collision. CSAA, with vastly more resources, had not bothered to do the same in the twenty-one days before declaring its investigation "concluded."

This is the first lie. It was institutional. It was pre-litigation. And it set the anchor from which every subsequent fraud was measured.

### The Lawyers

**Phillips, Spallas & Angstadt LLP (PSA)** is the defense firm that CSAA retained as successor counsel after the original defense team was replaced. PSA substituted into the case on May 4, 2023, taking over the defense file from prior counsel. The file contained whatever materials prior defense counsel had received during discovery -- including the Dolan production that contained the 911 audio, body-worn camera footage, and CAD logs.

**Priya D. Navaratnasingham** is the PSA attorney who signed the Motions in Limine, made the hearing representations to Judge Wong, and uttered the fatal admission on April 16, 2025. She is the person who told the court that the evidence was "news to me" and that the defense had not received the 911 call until the day of the mandatory settlement conference. She is also the person who, weeks later, casually told the same court that the body-worn camera footage was "provided by Dolan" and that she had curated five videos down to eight or nine minutes for the jury. She is the author of both the lie and its confession.

**Alberto Reyna** is the PSA attorney who, on February 1, 2024, issued a Compex deposition subpoena to the San Francisco Police Department specifically requesting the 911 call and body-worn camera footage by name. He is also the person who, at trial, claimed he was "not sure what recording" Rosario was referring to when Rosario tried to play the 911 call. He knew exactly what recording Rosario was referring to. He had subpoenaed it by name.

**Michael R. Chambers** and the firm **Carbone, Smith & Koyama** are the original defense counsel who received the Dolan production during discovery and transferred the defense file to PSA on May 4, 2023. Their role in the story is the chain-of-custody link: they received the evidence, they held it, and when they transferred the file, the evidence traveled with it. Whether they indexed it, flagged it, or buried it is a question that depositions will answer.

### The Judge

**Judge Garrett L. Wong** presided over Department 504 of the San Francisco Superior Court. He is not alleged to be corrupt. He is alleged to have been deceived. On February 18, 2025, when Navaratnasingham told him the evidence was "news to me," he responded with three words that broke the trial: **"I see. Okay."** Those three words were the sound of a court accepting a representation from an officer of the court at face value. He did not order an inquiry. He did not demand a declaration. He did not ask Navaratnasingham to reconcile her denial with the sworn testimony he had just heard from Jeremy Jessup. He accepted what she told him because the system presumes that lawyers tell the truth to judges. That presumption is the foundation of the adversary system. It is also what Navaratnasingham exploited.

### The Former Counsel

**Jeremy Jessup** of the **Dolan Law Firm** is the attorney who originally represented Rosario and who, within three months of the collision, obtained the 911 recording, body-worn camera footage, and CAD logs through routine public records requests. He is the attorney who produced those materials to defense counsel during discovery. He is the attorney who used them in the deposition of the defendant. And he is the attorney who, on February 18, 2025, took the stand and testified under oath that the materials had been "produced to defense counsel" and "served on them in the production for discovery." His testimony was uncontradicted. No one has attempted to impeach it. It is the pinwheel on which the entire fraud case turns.

## THE DENY-DELAY-DEFEND ARCHITECTURE: HOW CSAA STRUCTURED THE COVER-UP

The narrative will devote substantial attention to the institutional mechanics of CSAA's conduct. The deny-delay-defend model is not unique to this case. It is the operational playbook of the American insurance industry, documented by scholars, regulators, and investigative journalists. What this case provides is a granular, record-documented instance of how the model works in practice -- from the first denial letter to the courtroom fraud.

### Stage 1: Deny

CSAA's February 25, 2021 denial letter is the originating act. Issued twenty-one days after the collision, the letter states the investigation is "concluded" and the insured is "not liable." The investigation was not concluded. The insurer had not obtained the 911 recording, body-worn camera footage, or CAD dispatch records -- the three foundational pieces of evidence in any automobile collision case. The denial was issued before the insurer had reviewed the evidence that would determine liability.

The purpose of the early denial is coercive. A letter from a multi-billion-dollar institution telling an injured person that the investigation is "concluded" and the insured "not liable" is designed to discourage pursuit of the claim. It shifts the burden of evidence gathering from the insurer (which has the resources) to the claimant (who typically does not). It forces the claimant into litigation as the only mechanism for obtaining what the insurer should have gathered before issuing the denial. The early denial is not an investigative conclusion. It is a strategic weapon.

### Stage 2: Delay

Once litigation is filed, the institutional strategy shifts from denial to delay. Discovery extends over years. Depositions are taken. Motions are filed. Continuances are sought and granted. The asymmetry is structural: the insurer has unlimited legal resources and treats litigation costs as a cost of doing business. The claimant -- especially a brain-injured pro per claimant -- faces the accumulating burden of time, money, and cognitive overhead. Every month of delay increases the pressure on the claimant to settle for less than the claim is worth.

In this case, the delay extended from 2021 through trial in April 2025 -- more than four years. During that time, the original defense counsel was replaced. The defense file was transferred. The materials that would prove liability sat in the defense file, untouched by the new legal team for twenty months. The delay was not neutral. It was the medium through which the second lie became possible: by the time the case reached trial, the institutional memory of what had been produced in discovery had diffused across two law firms, and the opportunity to claim ignorance presented itself.

### Stage 3: Defend (Through Fraud)

The defend stage is where this case departs from the standard deny-delay-defend model and enters the territory of true crime. The defense did not merely defend the case aggressively. The defense lied to the judge.

The nineteen Motions in Limine filed on January 17, 2025, were the opening move. MIL No. 17, signed by Navaratnasingham and Phillips, targeted the 911 recording with three specific false predicates: the materials were "not produced during discovery"; the defendant had "no idea" where they came from; the defendant had "no idea" who created them. Each predicate was false. The materials had been produced to defense counsel during discovery. The defense had received them from the Dolan Law Firm. The defense had had them for nearly two years.

The February 18, 2025 hearing was the escalation. When Jessup testified under oath that the materials had been produced to defense counsel, Navaratnasingham did not request a recess to investigate. She did not file a supplemental declaration. She denied. "News to me." "Did not receive until the MSC." "Not produced in the normal course of discovery." These were not cautious qualifications. They were affirmative representations to a judge, made by an officer of the court, designed to induce specific rulings. The judge relied on them. The judge denied the continuance. The judge maintained the exclusionary framework. The stage was set for a trial in which the most powerful evidence of liability would be suppressed.

The trial itself was the culmination. Rosario entered the courtroom without depositions of the officers, without authenticated clips ready for the jury, without a coherent presentation strategy for the most important evidence in the case. He was a pro per plaintiff with a brain injury, operating under time and resource constraints that had been manufactured by the defense's fraud. The jury never received the evidence in the form, at the time, or with the context it should have had. The 911 recording arrived late and fragmented. The body-worn camera footage was presented by the defense on the defense's terms. The supplemental police report was excluded entirely. The governing statutory duty of care was categorically withheld from the jury that specifically asked for it.

The verdict was 9-3. The minimum civil majority. One juror's mind, changed by any single piece of suppressed evidence, would have altered the outcome.

"Did not receive until MSC" was a lie. "No idea where it came from" was a lie. Every exclusionary ruling, every continuance denial, every procedural constraint that flowed from those lies was the product of fraud on the court.

But no one in the courtroom stopped. The trial continued. The moment passed in the flow of proceedings. Rosario, managing his own case with a brain injury, did not have co-counsel to catch the contradiction in real time.

## STRUCTURAL AND THEMATIC NOTES

### The Epistemic Standard

This novel is built on an epistemic standard of empirical verifiability. Every factual claim must be traceable to a specific record source. The narrative does not speculate about motivations it cannot prove. It does not reconstruct conversations that are not in the transcript. It does not attribute thoughts or feelings to people whose inner states are not documented. Where the record is silent, the narrative says so.

This epistemic discipline serves two purposes. First, it protects the narrative from the defamation risks inherent in writing about living people and ongoing litigation.
