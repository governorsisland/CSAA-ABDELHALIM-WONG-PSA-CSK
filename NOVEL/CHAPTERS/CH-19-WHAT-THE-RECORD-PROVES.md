## Narrative

The Reliance Matrix: seven links. Each false representation produced a specific court ruling. Each ruling foreclosed a specific avenue of proof. Link 1: MIL 17 "not produced," "no idea" -- 911 excluded. Link 2: MIL 6 blanket exclusion. Link 3: "News to me" -- court accepted without inquiry. Link 4: "Did not have until MSC" -- continuance denied. Link 5: "Not produced in normal course" -- no judicial inquiry. Link 6: "Not received via subpoena" -- supplemental report excluded. Link 7: "Not sure what recording" -- 911 deferred. The proof that every link was false: RT 7, p. 214, ll. 13-15. "Sure. They were provided by Dolan. He has had them for whatever, sure." A single admission destroys the factual predicate of all seven representations. The verdict was 9-3. The defense did not win because the case was stronger. It won because lawyers lied and the judge believed them.

The certified transcript of the proceedings in this case is the most important document in the litigation. Not because it is the most eloquent, or the most legally sophisticated, or the most persuasive piece of writing. Because it is the record of what actually happened, in the words of the people who were there, preserved in the officially sanctioned form that courts treat as authoritative. The transcript cannot be amended, cannot be disputed, cannot be explained away. It is the baseline against which all subsequent claims must be measured. It says what it says.

What it says is this: before trial, defense counsel represented that the 911 recording and the body-worn camera footage had not been received through the normal course of discovery, were unknown to the defense, were not in the defense's possession until the day of the MSC, and were of uncertain provenance. During trial, defense counsel admitted that the body-worn camera footage had been "provided by Dolan." These two sets of statements are inconsistent. Both appear in the same certified transcript, in the words of the same lawyer, in the same proceeding. The transcript captures the lie and the confession.

### I. The Reliance Matrix in Full

The Reliance Matrix is the analytical instrument that converts the narrative of the fraud into a structured legal argument. It has seven rows, each documenting one link in the causal chain. Each row contains: the false representation (verbatim), the date, the transcript citation, the court ruling the representation produced, and the specific harm to Rosario's trial presentation. The matrix is not an opinion. It is a citation index. Every entry can be verified by reading the referenced page and line of the certified transcript.

Row 1: MIL No. 17. False representation: "The 911 call was not produced in the normal course of discovery" and "we have no idea where it came from." Date: the MIL filings preceding trial. The motion was premised on the false claim that the 911 recording had not been properly disclosed. Court ruling: the motion created an evidentiary constraint on the 911 recording's treatment at trial. Harm: the most powerful liability evidence was pre-marked as procedurally suspect before trial began.

Row 2: MIL No. 6. False representation: the blanket exclusionary motion premised on the false claim that Rosario had not properly disclosed the police materials. Date: MIL filings. Court ruling: exclusionary order. Harm: procedural constraints on Rosario's use of the materials at trial.

Row 3: "News to me." False representation: Navaratnasingham at the February 18 hearing, page 141, lines 4-11. "So this is news to me. We didn't realize that prior to handling counsel may have received it." Date: February 18, 2025. Transcript citation: hearing transcript, pp. 141, ll. 4-11. Court ruling: the court's acceptance of this representation as establishing the factual predicate for the subsequent rulings. Harm: the court's understanding of the evidence landscape was corrupted by this accepted false premise.

Row 4: "Did not have until MSC." False representation: hearing transcript, pages 163-165. "I am representing to this Court that we did not have those two items until the day of the MSC." Date: February 18, 2025, after recess. Court ruling: continuance denied at page 181. Harm: Rosario could not depose officers, authenticate clips through custodial testimony, or prepare a coherent evidentiary presentation.

Row 5: Reframing of Jessup's testimony. False representation: hearing transcript, pages 176-177. Navaratnasingham characterized Jessup's testimony about the defense's receipt of the Dolan production as being about Rosario's possession timeline. Date: February 18, 2025. Harm: the court's basis for the continuance denial incorporated a false characterization of the only sworn testimony in the record on the possession question.

Row 6: "Not received via subpoena." False representation: RT 7, around line 2874. "We subpoenaed the police records for the incident and this is not anything that we received as part of the subpoena." Date: April 16, 2025, Trial Day 7. Court ruling: "It is not evidence." Harm: supplemental police report excluded.

Row 7: "Not sure what recording." False representation: RT 7, lines 2990-2994. "I am not sure what recording he is referring to." Date: April 16, 2025, Trial Day 7. Court ruling: deferral of 911 recording admission pending Rosario's testimony. Harm: evidentiary sequence disrupted; 911 recording arrived late, out of context.

### II. The Single Admission That Collapses All Seven Links

At RT 7, page 214, lines 13-15, Navaratnasingham answered the court's question about the body-worn camera footage: "Sure. They were provided by Dolan. He has had them for whatever, sure."

The admission collapses all seven links. Jessup testified at hearing transcript pages 139-140 that the Dolan production included body cam footage, 911 audio, and CAD log, all produced together. If the footage was "provided by Dolan," the 911 recording was too. Under *Jackson v. County of Los Angeles* (1997) 60 Cal.App.4th 171, 183, judicial estoppel prevents asserting a position contrary to one successfully asserted earlier. The admission contradicts every prior representation.

Therefore: "News to me" was false. The body-worn camera footage from Dolan was known. "Did not have until MSC" was false. PSA had the body-worn camera footage from Dolan before the MSC. "No idea where it came from" was false. It came from Dolan. "Not produced in normal course of discovery" was false. Dolan produced it to defense counsel in the course of discovery. "Not received via subpoena" was an artifice. The materials were received through the Dolan production, not the defense's own subpoena. "Not sure what recording" was an artifice. Reyna had subpoenaed that recording by name fourteen months earlier.

All seven links. Destroyed by a single six-word admission.

### III. The Verdict in Context

The nine-to-three verdict is not the product of a fair adversary hearing. It is the product of seven documented false representations that corrupted the procedural framework, fragmented the evidentiary presentation, and denied the jury the unified picture that the full record would have provided. The defendant had made contact, in his own words: "I barely hit him." The defendant's own admission was authentic, in his own voice: "Yes. That is my voice." The governing statutory duty required him to yield: Vehicle Code sections 21950(a) and 22107. Every element of liability was established in the record. The verdict of nine to three in favor of the defense was not a finding that the plaintiff's case was weak. It was the product of a corrupted proceeding.

The defense did not win because the case was stronger. It won because lawyers lied and the judge believed them. The judge believed them because the system trusts its officers, and the trust the system extends to attorneys is the mechanism through which extrinsic fraud operates. The court said "I see. Okay" because it trusted Navaratnasingham. She betrayed that trust. The plaintiff paid the price. The record proves it. Every word of it.
