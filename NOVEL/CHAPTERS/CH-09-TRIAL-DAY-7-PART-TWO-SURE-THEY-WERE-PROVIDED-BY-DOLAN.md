# CHAPTER 9: Trial Day 7, Part Two — "Sure. They Were Provided by Dolan."

---

## Narrative

Minutes after the supplemental report exclusion, in the same session of Trial Day 7, the court asked a question about the body-worn camera footage that appeared on the defense's exhibit list. The question was routine. The court wanted to understand the source of what the defense was planning to present. Navaratnasingham responded without hesitation, without qualification, without any of the careful verbal hedging she had used in every prior context where possession of this material was at issue. At RT 7, page 214, lines 13-15: "Sure. They were provided by Dolan. He has had them for whatever, sure."

Four words from the 911 recording had been the evidentiary pivot of the entire case. Now six words from defense counsel had become the legal pivot of the fraud case. "Provided by Dolan." Three words. The confession that undid everything. The [Trial Day 7 transcript](https://github.com/soltrinox/CSAA-ABDELHALIM-WONG-PSA-CSK/blob/main/05-TRANSCRIPTS/REPORTER-TRANSCRIPTS/Trial-Day-07-April-16-2025.pdf) at RT 7, p. 214, ll. 13-15 preserves the admission. The [Fatal Proof document](https://github.com/soltrinox/CSAA-ABDELHALIM-WONG-PSA-CSK/blob/main/FATAL-PROOF-NAVARATNASINGHAM-DOLAN-ADMISSION-EXTRINSIC-FRAUD.md) catalogs every contradicting statement against every prior representation. The transcript speaks for itself.

### I. The Question and Its Context

The body-worn camera footage from SFPD officers at the scene of the February 4, 2021 collision was one of the most visually compelling pieces of evidence in the case. The footage captured the scene in the immediate aftermath of the impact. The officers' body cameras recorded their interactions with Rosario, their observations of the physical environment, and details of the collision site that could not be captured in written documents. This footage was part of the Dolan production. Jessup had testified at the February 18 hearing that the Dolan firm had produced "body cam footage, about five videos" to defense counsel as part of the discovery production. The footage was served on prior defense counsel, used in depositions, and transferred to PSA when the substitution occurred.

The defense had curated the footage for trial presentation. Navaratnasingham's statement that the defense had pared the footage down from five videos to "eight or nine minutes" for the jury revealed the extent of the defense's engagement with the material. They had not merely received the footage passively; they had reviewed it, selected portions, edited it to the segments they considered most useful for their purposes, and prepared it for presentation. This is not the behavior of a law firm that discovered evidence at the last minute. This is the behavior of a trial team that had been working with the material for a sufficient period to make considered editorial judgments about what to show the jury.

The court's question about the source of the footage was therefore not a difficult question. Navaratnasingham knew the source. She had always known the source. The source was Dolan. The question was whether she would say so.

---

### II. The Admission: What It Communicates

The text at RT 7, p. 214, ll. 13-15: "Sure. They were provided by Dolan. He has had them for whatever, sure."

"Sure" signals confidence and familiarity, not uncertainty. "They were provided by Dolan" is categorical: the Dolan Law Firm was the source. "He has had them for whatever, sure" indicates extended possession. This is the language of settled, recognized fact. As *Jackson v. County of Los Angeles* (1997) 60 Cal.App.4th 171, 183 holds, judicial estoppel prevents a party from asserting a position in one phase of a proceeding that is contrary to a position successfully asserted in another. The admission contradicted every prior representation about non-receipt. The two positions cannot coexist.

---

### III. The Prior Representations and Their Collapse

Set the admission against the record of prior representations. The [Fatal Proof document](https://github.com/soltrinox/CSAA-ABDELHALIM-WONG-PSA-CSK/blob/main/FATAL-PROOF-NAVARATNASINGHAM-DOLAN-ADMISSION-EXTRINSIC-FRAUD.md) catalogues every contradicting statement. Here is the core chain:

MIL No. 17, filed before trial: The defense represented that the 911 recording had "not been produced in the normal course of discovery" and that they had "no idea where it came from." The filing was the basis for the motion to exclude the 911 recording as improperly disclosed.

The February 18 hearing, hearing transcript pages 141-165: Navaratnasingham represented, under the court's direct attention, that "our office did not receive the 911 call until the day of the MSC." At pages 163-165: "I am representing to this Court that we did not have those two items until the day of the MSC."

Trial Day 7, RT 7, p. 214, ll. 13-15: "Sure. They were provided by Dolan."

The 911 call and the body-worn camera footage were part of the same Dolan production. Jessup's unimpeached testimony established this: "body cam footage, about five videos, as well as the 911 audio, as well as the CAD log" were produced to defense counsel together, in the same discovery response, used together in the depositions. They are not separable. If the body-worn camera footage was "provided by Dolan," then so was the 911 recording. The claim that the defense did not receive the 911 call through the Dolan production is directly contradicted by the admission that the body-worn camera footage, part of the same production, was received through exactly that channel.

"News to me" was false. It had never been "news." Navaratnasingham had known about the body-worn camera footage from Dolan from the time PSA took over the file. If she knew about the body-worn camera footage from Dolan, she was in constructive possession of the entire Dolan production. If she was in constructive possession of the entire Dolan production, "we did not receive the 911 call until the day of the MSC" was false. Every motion premised on that claim was built on a false predicate. Every ruling that adopted that premise was procured by a false statement.

---

### IV. The Moment That Passed

No one in the courtroom stopped. The trial continued. The court moved on to the next question. The jury was not present for this sidebar discussion. Rosario, representing himself with a traumatic brain injury, managing exhibits and witnesses and objections and procedure simultaneously, did not have a co-counsel available to catch the significance of the admission in real time and press the court for immediate consequences.

This is one of the structural injuries of the fraud. A represented plaintiff with a litigation team would have had a second attorney whose only job, in that moment, would have been to sit behind the first chair and track every statement for contradictions, cross-references, and strategic implications. When Navaratnasingham said "provided by Dolan," a second attorney would have had the prior representations flagged, ready to confront the contradiction immediately, to demand that the court take notice, to seek immediate remediation of every ruling that had been premised on the "did not receive" narrative. Rosario had no second attorney. He had himself. He was managing the presentation of his own evidence, the examination of witnesses, the response to objections, and the real-time processing of a complex proceeding, all with a brain injury.

The significance of the admission was not lost on him. It was recognized. It was documented. It became the anchor of the fraud case that he would build after the verdict. But in real time, at Trial Day 7, the admission passed without correction. The court did not sua sponte revisit the MIL rulings. The body-worn camera footage remained on the defense's exhibit list, to be presented on the defense's terms, edited to eight or nine minutes, without the context that the full production and Jessup's testimony would have provided.

The fraud had already done its work. The suppression of evidence, the deferred admission, the excluded supplemental report, the denied continuance: each of these had occurred before the trial day on which the admission was made. By the time Navaratnasingham said "provided by Dolan," the evidentiary structure of Rosario's case had already been compromised by the rulings that the false representations had procured. The admission did not undo the harm. It only proved it.

The admission is the cornerstone of three independent proceedings. In the equity action, it proves the representations were false. As *Kulchar v. Kulchar* (1969) 1 Cal.3d 467, 471 holds, extrinsic fraud on the court can warrant vacatur of a judgment through an independent action in equity when the fraud prevented the losing party from presenting its case. In the fraud action, the admission proves the misrepresentations were knowing. In the bar complaint, it proves violation of Business and Professions Code section 6068(d). The [Fatal Proof document](https://github.com/soltrinox/CSAA-ABDELHALIM-WONG-PSA-CSK/blob/main/FATAL-PROOF-NAVARATNASINGHAM-DOLAN-ADMISSION-EXTRINSIC-FRAUD.md) builds the complete chain. The transcript speaks. "Sure. They were provided by Dolan."
