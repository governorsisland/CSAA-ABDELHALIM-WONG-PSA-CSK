### I. The Certified Record

The certified Reporter's Transcript is not merely a document. It is a representation of what actually happened in a courtroom, in the presence of a judge, captured by a trained court reporter and certified as accurate. It is the authoritative record of the proceeding. Courts treat it as such. Appeals are argued from the certified transcript. Fraud-on-court claims are built from the certified transcript. Bar complaints are supported by the certified transcript. The transcript is the baseline against which all subsequent claims about what happened in the proceeding are measured.

Set A: "news to me," "did not receive until MSC," "no idea where it came from." Set B: "Sure. They were provided by Dolan. He has had them for whatever, sure." Both in the same certified transcript, same attorney, same materials. They cannot coexist. Under *Jackson v. County of Los Angeles* (1997) 60 Cal.App.4th 171, 183, judicial estoppel prevents asserting a position contrary to one successfully asserted earlier. The truth of Set B establishes that Set A was false.

---

### II. The Binary Contradiction

The binary contradiction between Set A and Set B is the logical core of the fraud case. It is not an inference or a reconstruction. It is a direct contradiction between two sets of statements in the same document, by the same speaker, about the same subject matter. No interpretation is required. No inference is needed. The statements cannot both be true. This is the simplest kind of proof: the defendant's own words prove the falsity of her own earlier words.

The binary contradiction is also the reason no defense position can neutralize the fraud claim. Any defense of the pre-trial representations requires explaining them away in the face of the Trial Day 7 admission. Any defense of the Trial Day 7 admission requires acknowledging possession of the materials, which directly contradicts the pre-trial representations. There is no narrative that reconciles Set A and Set B while maintaining that the representations were made in good faith.

The candidates for reconciliation have all been foreclosed. The "we received it later" narrative is foreclosed by the "provided by Dolan" characterization: the body-worn camera footage was not received after the MSC through a newly discovered channel. It was "provided by Dolan," which refers to the Dolan Law Firm's prior production to defense counsel, not to any post-MSC delivery. The "I was confused about which materials" narrative is foreclosed by the specificity of both sets of statements: Set A specifically described the 911 call and the body-worn camera footage, and Set B specifically described the body-worn camera footage that was "provided by Dolan." There is no reconciliation. The fraud is proven by the transcript itself.

---

### III. The Transcript as Protagonist

This book takes its title from the final chapter: the transcript speaks. In a legal narrative, the protagonist is usually a person: the plaintiff who suffered the wrong, the defendant who caused it, the attorney who committed the fraud, the judge who accepted the false representations. In this story, the true protagonist is the transcript. It is the document that carries the full weight of the case. Every legal claim rests on it. Every factual assertion is drawn from it. Every remedy is founded on what it says.

The transcript captured the lie in the formal, official record of the proceeding. The lie was made to the court. The court relied on it. The reliance produced the rulings that corrupted the trial. All of this is in the transcript. The transcript also captured the confession: "Sure. They were provided by Dolan." The confession was made in open court, in the same proceeding, on the same record. The transcript holds both. It does not editorialize. It does not advocate. It records. Verba scripta manent: written words endure.

The transcript is also the mechanism of accountability. It is the exhibit in the equity action, the evidence in the fraud action, the basis for the bar complaints, the record on appeal. Every proceeding that seeks to address the fraud draws its authority from the transcript. The transcript is not just a record of what happened. It is the instrument by which what happened will be corrected.

---

### IV. What Comes Next

The proceedings are now pending. The appeal in A173827 is in the briefing phase. The equity action in CGC-25-631801 is in the early litigation phase. The fraud action in CGC-25-631802 is proceeding. The bar complaints are filed. The judicial complaint is filed. The $75 million demand expires on May 6, 2026.

The transcript speaks. It has been speaking since April 16, 2025, when Navaratnasingham said "Sure. They were provided by Dolan," and every word of every prior false representation became, in that moment, documented fraud on the court. The speaking is not rhetorical. It is legal: each word is a citation, each citation is a link in the causal chain, each link establishes an element of the legal theory that the three fronts are advancing. The record will determine whether justice is done or the fraud stands.

Rosario's position is simple. He does not ask the court to accept his interpretation of ambiguous events. He does not ask for credibility judgments between equally plausible accounts. He asks only that the court read the certified transcript. Set A and Set B. The contradiction that no explanation can reconcile. The fraud that the transcript itself proves. The harm that flowed from it. The remedies that the law provides. The transcript speaks. The question is whether those who have the power to act on what it says will do so.

The collision at the crosswalk on February 4, 2021 lasted a fraction of a second. The four words spoken into the 911 dispatcher's phone, "I barely hit him," took less than two seconds to say. What followed from those two seconds, the denial, the litigation, the fraud, the verdict, the three proceedings that are now unfolding, has consumed five years and will consume more. The transcript of those five years, every hearing, every trial day, every ruling, every admission, speaks clearly and permanently about what happened and why. The transcript is the protagonist. And unlike the other participants in this story, the transcript will not change its account. It recorded the truth. The truth endures. Verba scripta manent.
