# CHAPTER 10: "I Am Not Sure What Recording He Is Referring To"

---

## Narrative

When Rosario attempted to play the 911 recording at trial, defense counsel Alberto Reyna rose and objected. His words, preserved at Reporter's Transcript, Trial Day 7, lines 2990-2994: "I am not sure what recording he is referring to."

Alberto Reyna had, on February 1, 2024, issued a [Compex subpoena](https://github.com/soltrinox/CSAA-ABDELHALIM-WONG-PSA-CSK/blob/main/04-EXHIBITS/2024-02-01-Compex-Subpoena.pdf) to the San Francisco Police Department. The subpoena, identified by Order No. CA1147102-021, specifically requested the 911 call and body-worn camera footage related to the February 4, 2021 incident by name, by description, by case number. He was not uncertain about the existence of a 911 recording related to this incident on February 1, 2024. He had directed a professional process service company to subpoena that specific recording by name from the specific government agency that possessed it.

Fourteen months later, he told the court: "I am not sure what recording he is referring to."

This is not confusion. This is not a lapse of memory. This is an artifice. Business and Professions Code section 6068(d) prohibits an attorney from seeking "to mislead the judge or any judicial officer by an artifice or false statement of fact or law." The claim of uncertainty about the identity of the 911 recording, made by the attorney who had specifically subpoenaed that recording fourteen months earlier, is an artifice in the statutory sense: a device designed to mislead, to create the impression that Rosario was attempting to present some unidentifiable or improperly disclosed piece of audio without foundation or provenance.

The court deferred admission of the 911 call. Rosario would have to testify first. RT 7, lines 3024-3057. The ruling, made in reliance on the defense's posture of uncertainty, reorganized the evidentiary sequence in ways that damaged Rosario's presentation. Instead of hearing the defendant's own words, authenticated and in context, early in the liability case, the jury would receive the 911 recording out of sequence, stripped of the corroborating body-worn camera footage and supplemental report, fragmented and decontextualized. The most powerful single piece of liability evidence in the case was being managed by the adversary that had spent months telling the court it did not exist.

---

### I. The 911 Recording as Evidentiary Anchor

The 911 call was the evidentiary anchor of the liability case. The defendant's own words, spoken at the scene before any lawyer had coached him. "I barely hit him." Under Evidence Code section 1220, a party admission is not hearsay. The defense theory was that contact was minimal or nonexistent. "I barely hit him" establishes contact; "barely" qualifies force, not existence. A pedestrian struck at a crosswalk, even slightly, was struck in violation of Vehicle Code section 21950(a). The admission establishes the predicate fact through the defendant's own mouth, immune to credibility challenges.

---

### II. Reyna's Objection: The Compex Subpoena Contradiction

Reyna had authorized Compex subpoena Order No. CA1147102-021 to the SFPD on February 1, 2024, specifically requesting the 911 call and body-worn camera footage. The subpoena required enough particularity to direct the agency to locate those records. Fourteen months later he told the court he was "not sure what recording" Rosario meant. The claim of uncertainty is documented as false by his own prior conduct. Business and Professions Code section 6068(d) prohibits artifice or false statements to mislead the court. The [Compex subpoena](https://github.com/soltrinox/CSAA-ABDELHALIM-WONG-PSA-CSK/blob/main/04-EXHIBITS/2024-02-01-Compex-Subpoena.pdf) (Order No. CA1147102-021) contradicts the objection.

---

### III. The Court's Deferral and Its Consequences

The court, at RT 7, lines 3024-3057, deferred admission of the 911 call. Rosario would need to testify first; then the recording could be offered in connection with his testimony. The ruling was procedurally motivated, based in part on the defense's expressed uncertainty about the recording's identity, and in part on the court's management of the evidentiary sequence in a trial that was proceeding under conditions of compressed preparation and limited foundation witnesses.

The deferral reversed the planned sequence: testimony before admission instead of admission before testimony. The jury would hear Rosario's account before the defendant's words that corroborated it. The supplemental report was excluded; the body-worn camera footage was presented on the defense's terms. The unified evidentiary picture was systematically fragmented by rulings procured through false representations.

---

### IV. The Causal Chain: Link 7

This chapter documents Link 7 in the Reliance Matrix: the seventh link in the causal chain connecting the fraud to the harm. False representation (Reyna's claim of uncertainty about the 911 recording's identity) → court ruling (deferral of admission pending Rosario's testimony, RT 7, ll. 3024-3057) → deprivation (disruption of the evidentiary sequence; 911 recording arrives late, out of sequence, stripped of corroborating context).

The harm from Link 7 compounds the harms from Links 1 through 6. The continuance denial (Link 1) had prevented Rosario from deposing officers and authenticating the footage through custodial testimony. The MIL rulings (Links 2-5) had imposed procedural constraints on how he could use the materials at trial. The supplemental report exclusion (Link 6) had eliminated the corroborating police narrative. Now the deferral of the 911 recording (Link 7) disrupted the evidentiary sequence that Rosario had planned to establish the liability case.

Each link is individually documented in the trial transcript. Each link flows directly from a false representation or an artifice by defense counsel. The cumulative effect of seven links is the systematic dismantling of Rosario's evidentiary case, not by lawful adversarial contest but by the corruption of the judicial process through false statements to the court. The [Trial Day 7 transcript](https://github.com/soltrinox/CSAA-ABDELHALIM-WONG-PSA-CSK/blob/main/05-TRANSCRIPTS/REPORTER-TRANSCRIPTS/Trial-Day-07-April-16-2025.pdf) preserves the exchange. The [Compex subpoena](https://github.com/soltrinox/CSAA-ABDELHALIM-WONG-PSA-CSK/blob/main/04-EXHIBITS/2024-02-01-Compex-Subpoena.pdf) documents the knowledge that made the claim of uncertainty false. Together, they prove that "I am not sure what recording he is referring to" was an artifice designed to mislead the court about the identity, provenance, and admissibility of the most powerful piece of evidence in the case.
